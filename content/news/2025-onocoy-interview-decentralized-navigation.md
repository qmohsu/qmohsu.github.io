**Lucy Icking** of **[onocoy](https://onocoy.com)** visited Prof. Li-Ta Hsu — head of the Intelligent Positioning and Navigation Lab — at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University to explore the future of **GNSS correction and decentralized navigation infrastructure**. ([Lucy Icking](/people/lucy-icking/) is also a visiting PhD student in IPNL, from Hannover University, Germany.)

A leading voice in urban GNSS innovation, Prof. Hsu's work bridges cutting-edge research and real-world applications — from RTK and GNSS–LiDAR fusion to autonomous driving and smart agriculture — informed by a background spanning UCL, the University of Tokyo, and Google's Android Location Team.

The conversation covers:

- Why GNSS is critical infrastructure — and how most people don't even realize it
- The challenges of precision positioning in urban and global environments
- How correction networks and decentralized models like onocoy can unlock global use cases
- The role East Asia can play in the future of robotics and autonomous systems
