Prof. Li-Ta Hsu was invited to speak at the **Hong Kong Motor Transport and Driving Industry Workers General Union** (香港汽車交通運輸業總工會) forum titled *「智能駕駛浪潮下　汽車交通運輸業何去何從」* (*The Intelligent Driving Wave: Where is the Motor Transport Industry Heading*) on 12 July 2025, held at Hong Kong Wollongong College's lecture hall.

The forum brought together **over 100 representatives** from government, industry, academia, and labor, addressing how autonomous driving technology will affect the Hong Kong transport workforce — job displacement, worker retraining, transition planning, and the need for worker protection in the autonomous-vehicle era.

**Other speakers at the forum included:**

- Wu Qiubei, Chair, Federation of Hong Kong Industries
- Ho Kai-ming, Deputy Secretary for Labour and Welfare
- Chung Cheuk-ming, Senior Mechanical Engineer, Transport Department
- So Tung-tse, Smart Mobility Director, Hong Kong Applied Science and Technology Research Institute
- Lee Yiu-pui, Honorary Chair, Hong Kong Automobile Industry Association
- Li Xiaoliang, General Manager, Shenzhen United Aircraft Technology

Prof. Hsu's talk bridged the lab's academic research on intelligent vehicle positioning (including the Vehicular Self-diagnosis System for Intelligent Transport line of work) with the practical concerns of the Hong Kong transport workforce. Union leader Lam Wai-kong emphasized at the forum that development *"must be people-centric"* and called for detailed worker placement and transition plans alongside any autonomous-vehicle rollout.

Engagements like this extend the lab's public-community service line alongside the recurring 香港青少年航天創新大賽 (HK Youth Aerospace Innovation Competition) judging role.
