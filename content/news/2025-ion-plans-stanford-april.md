In April 2025, PolyU's [Intelligent Positioning and Navigation Laboratory (IPNL)](https://www.polyu-ipn-lab.com/) had a strong presence at the [2025 IEEE/ION Position, Location, and Navigation Symposium (PLANS)](https://www.ion.org/plans/) in Salt Lake City, Utah (28 April – 1 May 2025), with three lab papers in the technical program. The trip included a Stanford University visit on the way to Utah.

**Three IPNL papers at PLANS 2025:**

- Li Z., Yan P., Wen W., **Hsu L.T.** — *Paired Cauchy-Gaussian overbound for not symmetric unimodal heavy-tailed distributions* (PLANS 2025 pp. 214–225)
- Song B., Wen W., **Hsu L.T.** — *Certifiably Optimal Satellite Orbit Determination Based on Doppler Measurements for Low-Earth-Orbit Satellites* (PLANS 2025 pp. 410–416)
- Zhong Y., Ng H.F., **Hsu L.T.**, Wen W. — *GNSS/Map Integration with Batch Factor Graph Optimization for Reliable Trajectory Estimation in Urban Canyon* (PLANS 2025 pp. 1411–1416)

PLANS is the [IEEE Aerospace and Electronic Systems Society](https://ieee-aess.org/)'s biennial navigation flagship, jointly organized with the [Institute of Navigation](https://www.ion.org/); the 2025 edition drew 340 participants. The Stanford leg of the trip extended the lab's ongoing engagement with the [Stanford GPS Lab](https://scpnt.stanford.edu/) following Prof. Hsu's first invited talk there in April 2023.
