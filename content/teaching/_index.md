The lab's teaching activities span undergraduate aviation engineering, GNSS-focused courses, and a multi-year research line on **GitHub-based pedagogy** for engineering education. The teaching arc has been recognized with the PolyU Presidential Award in Knowledge Transfer (2022-24) and an active HK$960k PolyU Online Teaching Development grant.

---

## Course: AAE2004 — Engineering Computation and Simulation

AAE2004 is a core undergraduate course in the Department of Aeronautical and Aviation Engineering, covering numerical methods, simulation, and software-engineering practice for engineering students. The course pioneered the use of **GitHub workflows** as the primary platform for student project management, version control, and code review — a teaching innovation that became the foundation of the lab's pedagogy research.

The student-facing tutorials below are part of the AAE2004 GitHub Project series, designed to onboard engineering students who have no prior software development experience.

## Research: GitHub as a Supplementary Educational Tool

The teaching innovation behind AAE2004 is also a research thread. The lab studies how GitHub workflows change student behavior, collaboration patterns, and learning outcomes in engineering courses.

- **ICLT 2025 paper** — *GitHub as a Supplementary Educational Tool* (Hsu, ICLT 2025, Hong Kong). See the [news post](/news/2025-iclt-github-pedagogy/) for details and a video demonstration of the method.
- Multi-year line builds on earlier PolyU Online Teaching Development grant work and URIS undergraduate research project supervision.
