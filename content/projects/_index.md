IPNL projects are not isolated demos. They are research platforms and programs that connect field data, mathematical models, open-source tools, receiver platforms, and real-world systems. We build datasets, algorithms, software libraries, and embodied systems to test navigation under the conditions where positioning is most likely to fail.

The page is organized into three groups:

- **A. Open Platforms and Benchmarks** — datasets, code, and receivers that other researchers can use directly.
- **B. Core Research Programs** — long-running funded research agendas behind the lab's flagship publications and patents.
- **C. Embodied and Translational Systems** — applied robotics, spinouts, and community initiatives that translate research into deployable systems.

For the full list of open-source releases (datasets, software, companion code), see the [Resources page](/resources/).
