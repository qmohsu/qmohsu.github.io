## For industry

The lab structures industry engagements in three layers, by depth of commitment.

<div class="layer-grid">
  <div class="layer-card">
    <div class="layer-tag">Layer 1 · framework partnership</div>
    <h3>Multi-year framework</h3>
    <p>24–36 month research agreements with named deliverables, joint personnel, and milestone reviews. This is how the <strong>Huawei</strong>, <strong>OPPO</strong>, <strong>AIROHA</strong>, and <strong>MediaTek</strong> engagements are run.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="layer-card">
    <div class="layer-tag">Layer 2 · funded research project</div>
    <h3>Scoped research project</h3>
    <p>12–24 month single-scope projects with a defined research question and contracted hours. Examples: Google Research Award (US$40k, 2024); ITF-ITSP (HK$801k, 2024).</p>
  </div>
  <div class="layer-card">
    <div class="layer-tag">Layer 3 · consultancy / advisory</div>
    <h3>Time-bounded advisory</h3>
    <p>A few weeks to a few months: integrity-monitoring frameworks, architecture review, technical due diligence, expert opinion. SAIC Motor runs in this mode.</p>
  </div>
</div>

### Time horizons

<div class="lane-grid lane-grid-2">
<div class="lane-col">
<div class="lane-col-tag">Fast lane &middot; can start quickly</div>

- Evaluate your algorithm on [UrbanNav](/projects/urbannav/)
- Technical Q&A call (30–60 min)
- Review a paper draft or proposal
- Invited talk on existing topics

</div>
<div class="lane-col">
<div class="lane-col-tag">Slow lane &middot; needs setup</div>

- Joint data collection campaign
- Co-supervised PhD student
- Multi-year framework partnership
- Custom dataset for your city

</div>
</div>

### How to start

[Send a message →](#contact) with:

- Who you are and your affiliation
- The nature of the collaboration and which layer above it fits
- Constraints — timing, location/timezone, budget if relevant
- Links to relevant material (technical brief, RFP, prior work)

See also: [R&D Projects](/projects/#partners) for a partner-by-partner view of how existing collaborations are structured.

</section>

## For academic collaborators

Joint publications and co-authored papers; dataset sharing and benchmark development; co-advised students (PhD, MPhil, visiting); joint grant proposals; workshop and conference co-organization. The lab co-chairs the **ION/IAG Working Group on Positioning at Asian Urban Canyons** and welcomes collaborators on the working-group track.

Particular openings in 2026 include cross-institutional projects with **Taiwan partners**, integrity-monitoring methodology exchanges, and joint dataset campaigns for new cities.

To propose academic collaboration, [send a message →](#contact) with a brief description of the proposed work and any timing constraints.

</section>

## For students, postdocs, and research assistants

<div class="layer-grid">
  <div class="layer-card">
    <div class="layer-tag">PhD students</div>
    <p>Strong quantitative background in GNSS, signal processing, robotics, or machine learning. Self-directed, comfortable with field experiments, eager to publish in top venues.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="layer-card">
    <div class="layer-tag">Postdoctoral fellows</div>
    <p>Track record of high-quality publications in positioning, navigation, or related areas. Interest in mentoring students and leading independent research threads.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="layer-card">
    <div class="layer-tag">Visiting researchers</div>
    <p>PhD students or early-career researchers from partner institutions interested in 3- to 12-month research visits.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="layer-card">
    <div class="layer-tag">Research assistants</div>
    <p>Engineering graduates interested in hands-on dataset collection, system integration, and algorithm development.</p>
  </div>
</div>

### Current interests (2026)

- **Urban GNSS benchmarking** — new cities, new sensors, new challenges
- **Integrity monitoring** — safety-critical applications and certification pathways
- **Factor graph optimization** — alternative fusion architectures to EKF
- **Embodied AI localization** — drones, robots, delivery vehicles
- **Taiwan partnerships** — joint projects and student exchanges

### How to apply

[Send a message →](#contact) with:

- Who you are (name, affiliation, role)
- What you want to do (1–2 sentences)
- Why you think IPNL is a good fit
- Your CV and relevant publications
- Timeline and constraints

For PhD applications, please also mention your funding situation (self-funded, seeking fellowship, etc.) and your preferred start date.

</section>
